Code wars
Event Organisers:
Contacts:


CODE-WARS is a flagship event of FlairFiesta which invites the competitive Programmers all around the nation to solve problems using their algorithmic skills. If your life is summarized by 4 the words – EAT, SLEEP, CODE, REPEAT? Want to prove yourself to be the ULTIMATE CODER? So, register yourself in this CODING MARATHON to prove your worth.

Code Wars
Participation fee: 100 INR
Rules:
1. The contest is open to anyone with a knack for programming.
2. It is a solo contest.
3. Contest will be held on HACKEREARTH PLATFORM.
4. Everyone will be ranked according to the number of problems solved and ties will be broken by the total time for each user in ascending order of time.
5. Total time is the sum of the time consumed for each problem
solved. The time consumed for a solved problem is the time elapsed from the beginning of the contest to the submission of the first accepted run plus 20 penalty minutes for every previously rejected run for that problem (Wrong answer, Time limit exceeded, Runtime Error, Compilation Error). There is no time consumed for a problem that is not solved.
6. The contest will consist of two rounds i.e. prelims (ONLINE) and FINAL ONSITE ROUND, only few individuals will be progressed to the final onsite round on the basis of their ranks.

Web-tags

Connect the Dots
Rules:

3.There are max. 2 members allowed per team.
4.The event will comprise of 2 rounds. Teams selected from the first round will proceed to the second round.

Contacts:


Reading between the lines is event to test your mental and technical acuity of your brain so get ready for the brain teasers like code snippets, multiple choice questions, program code writing/correction, questions based on electronics field,one word answer based on logical ,reasoning and technical stuff. Two separate events are there for both computer science and electronics and communication in which any one who is interested can participate.



Reading between the lines
Rules:
4.1 CS based quiz:
1.Event will be held in 2 Rounds.
2.Participating team can have maximum of two (2) members.
3.There will be mixed type of questions based on technical subjects,logic, miscellaneous.etc.
4.Any type of electronic devices will not be allowed during the event. If found using your team will be immediately disqualified.

Round 1:
This will be a written elimination round consisting of questions related to electronic fundamentals and hardware.
1.Question sheets will be provided to the registered teams on a particular time.
2.They will have 45 min. to solve the questions and submit the answer sheets.
3.Answers have to be written on the same sheet.
4.There will be no negative marking in questions. Details will be specified in question sheet.

4.2 Circuitrix:
1.There will be total 2 rounds.
2.Each team can have a max of 2 members.
3.First round will be elimination round with objective type questions from fields of electronics.
4.Second round will be analytical based.


Round 2: Question will be analytical based and
1.Exploration of concepts/theories.
2.general awareness regarding electronics.
Further details of this round will be provided in the question paper.

Datahulk
Event Organisers:
Contacts:


DataHulk is a data science competition, introduced this year in Flair Fiesta 2019 which encourages student to use tools of mathematics with power of computers to solve real world problems. If you think data is the next sexy thing and data science the next sexy science, the event will be right in your backyard.



Data Hulk
Rules:
1.The competition will be hosted on Kaggle, and as such a kaggle account is required for participation.
2.Students currently (as of 5th April 2019) pursuing bachelor’s/master’s degree in any of the technical field (like B.Tech, BCA, B.Sc, M.Tech, MCA, M.Sc in any branch) are eligible to participate.
3.The problem statement will be announced on 27 March, 2019.
4.Multiple submission to the problem statement are allowed.
5.The last date for submission of the solution on Kaggle will be 5th April 2019, 11:59 PM Indian Standard Time.
6.Participants needs to code the solution in Python/R, one may use any of the free of cost available libraries/framework/module such as numpy, tensorflow, sklearn, and etc.
7.Participant may be asked to submit script/notebook of solution any time after the first submission at organizer’s discretion.
